Malware Reference Guide

What is Malware?

Malware, short for “malicious software,” refers to any intrusive software made to steal data and damage or destroy computers and computer systems.

Types of Malware

Spyware

Spyware is software that runs in the background of a computer system that tracks the activity of a computer without the user knowing and allows the hacker to capture data. It runs as a background process and can be found through the OS task manager, although under a discrete name.

Example of Spyware

How to Prevent:

Avoid downloading from unknown or untrusted sources on the internet. Keep systems and applications up to date.

Adware

Adware is software that displays ads that may be malicious or slow down your computer or smart devices. Adware may be installed inadvertently through installation wizards.

Example of Adware

How to Prevent:

Take caution when going to websites where you download files. Ads may appear as misleading download buttons. In this case, use an adblocker to prevent this. For installation set-up wizards, do not select the option to install additional software.

Ransomware

Ransomware aims to lock users out of their system and prevent them from accessing their data until they pay a ransom.

Example of Adware

How to prevent:

Back up your data. Do not open e-mail links from unknown senders. Take caution when opening attachments from known senders by sending the attachment to a virus scanning tool. Avoid downloading from untrusted sources.
